Your database manager: lightweight, local, and free.

Native, without the weight of Electron or a JVM. Everything stays on your machine —no cloud, no accounts, no telemetry— with safe transactional editing for SQLite, MySQL/MariaDB, PostgreSQL, Informix, and MongoDB.

    A C core and the system WebView: starts instantly, without the weight of an Electron runtime or a JVM.

  • Multi-engine

    SQLite, MySQL/MariaDB, PostgreSQL, Informix, and MongoDB from one interface, with Informix support built in.

  • Local and private

    Your connections and queries stay on your machine. No accounts, no cloud, no telemetry.

  • Safe editing

    Free and auditable. No per-seat licenses or features behind a paywall.

Where the competition wins. Tabularis is a very mature, broad free client: 15+ engines via plugins, an AI assistant, visual EXPLAIN, and MCP. It shares Quaero's lightweight, local, free philosophy; today Quaero covers fewer engines but adds a server monitor, users/privileges, and export to more formats. DBeaver supports far more engines (80+ via JDBC) and years of maturity. Navicat is more polished on advanced features. Server Studio knows Informix inside out. phpMyAdmin needs nothing installed. Quaero doesn't aim to cover everything — just to be minimal and honest.
